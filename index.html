Transform Manual Cataloging into Automated Efficiency

For decades, auction houses, estate sellers, and catalog managers have faced the same challenge: turning a room full of items into an organized catalog that bidders and buyers can understand. The process has traditionally involved long hours of manual work — in addition to photographing each object: typing out titles, researching descriptions, and copy-pasting details from one window to another.

The Cataloging Challenge

Anyone who has ever worked an estate auction or managed a product catalog knows how quickly the hours add up. A single auction might contain hundreds of objects, and each one requires at least a basic entry. Experienced staff members can move quickly, but the human brain has limits. As fatigue sets in, errors creep into the process: typos, mistakes and missed items of value that slip through unnoticed because the cataloger simply ran out of time or energy.

This has always been the bottleneck of online cataloging. The manual process is tedious, repetitive, and error-prone. Every copy-paste from one window to another increases the chance of a mistake. Every late-night typing session increases the chance that something important will be overlooked. Even the best catalogers — people with years of experience and sharp eyes for detail — are still human, and human attention wears thin with fatigue and repetition.

Our Solution

Auctologue Catalog Assistant was created to relieve this burden. Instead of forcing you to research and copy-paste by hand, the extension automatically retrieves images directly from the page you are viewing. Those images are securely transmitted to our processing system, where they are analyzed and transformed into structured, catalog-ready entries. In just seconds, you receive professional titles, descriptions, and suggested categories — no switching between windows, no extra typing, no endless copy-paste.
