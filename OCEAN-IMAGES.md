## What to look for

### Seaweed / plants
- **Tall, vertical** shot of kelp or seaweed (underwater or dark blue/green).
- **Single plant or dense strip** so it still reads when cropped to a narrow column (about 220px wide, anchored at bottom).
- Works well if the **bottom is darker** and the **top fades or is simpler**, so it blends with the gradient.
- **Format:** JPG or PNG. Suggested size: at least 400px wide × 600px tall (or similar ratio).

### Fish
- **One fish per image** (or one clear main subject), side view.
- **Bright colors** (orange, yellow, blue, etc.) so they stand out on the blue background.
- **Format:** JPG works (current placeholders). For no background box, use a **PNG with transparent background** and name the file `fish1.png` etc., then update the `src` in `index.html`.
- Suggested size: about 200–500px on the longest side.

## Where to get good images

### Free (check license for your use)
- **Pexels** (pexels.com) — Search “underwater seaweed”, “kelp”, “tropical fish”, “fish PNG”. Filter by free stock; some fish are PNG with transparency.
- **Pixabay** (pixabay.com) — Same searches; many transparent PNGs for fish.
- **Unsplash** (unsplash.com) — Strong photos; fewer transparent fish. Use for seaweed; for fish you may need to crop or use a different source.
- **OpenGameArt / itch.io** — “Fish sprite”, “underwater” for game-style PNGs (often transparent).

### Paid (high quality, clear license)
- **Shutterstock / Adobe Stock** — Search “underwater seaweed”, “tropical fish transparent”, “fish PNG”.
- **Envato Elements** — Packs of “underwater PNG” or “fish sprite” with transparent assets.

### Make your own
- **Remove background:** Use [remove.bg](https://www.remove.bg) or similar on a fish photo to get a transparent PNG.
- **AI:** Generate “single tropical fish, side view, transparent background, PNG” and export as PNG.
